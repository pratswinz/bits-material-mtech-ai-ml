Mid-sem syllabus — topics & subtopics

From mid-sem PYQs (Dec 2025, Jan 2026, June 2026, 2025 papers) · Companion Sessions 1–7 up to sampling distribution of sample proportion

1 · Descriptive statistics

  • Data types — nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio
  • Mean, median, mode
  • Range, variance, standard deviation
  • Quartiles and IQR
  • Skew and symmetry
  • Five-number summary
  • Box plot
  • Outlier rule (1.5 × IQR)

2 · Probability basics

  • Sample space, outcome, event
  • Union, intersection, complement
  • Mutually exclusive events
  • Independent events
  • Probability axioms
  • Addition rule
  • Inclusion–exclusion (two & three sets)
  • Counting / equally likely outcomes

3 · Conditional probability

  • Conditional probability P(A|B)
  • Multiplication rule
  • Independence test
  • Total probability theorem
  • Probability tree diagrams

4 · Bayes & Naïve Bayes

  • Bayes theorem
  • Prior, likelihood, posterior
  • Total probability in Bayes denominator
  • Naïve Bayes classifier
  • Word likelihood tables
  • Laplace smoothing

5 · Random variables

  • Discrete vs continuous RV
  • PMF and validity rules
  • CDF
  • Expectation E[X]
  • Variance and standard deviation
  • Joint PMF / joint PDF
  • Marginal distributions
  • Independence of random variables

6 · Probability distributions

  • Bernoulli distribution
  • Binomial distribution
  • Poisson distribution
  • Normal distribution
  • Z-scores and standard normal table
  • 68–95–99.7 rule
  • Binomial → Poisson approximation
  • Binomial → Normal approximation
  • Continuity correction

7 · Sampling (up to sample proportion)

  • Population vs sample
  • Probability vs non-probability sampling
  • Simple random sampling
  • Stratified sampling
  • Systematic & cluster sampling
  • Sampling variation
  • Sampling distribution of sample mean (x̄)
  • Central Limit Theorem
  • Standard error σ/√n
  • CLT applications for sample mean
  • Finite population correction
  • Sample proportion p̂
  • Sampling distribution of sample proportion
  • CLT for proportions (np, nq conditions)
