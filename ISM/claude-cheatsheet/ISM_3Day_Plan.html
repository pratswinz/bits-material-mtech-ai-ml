ISM Mid-Sem — 3-Day Plan

← Hub · ~5–6 hrs/day · Theory → PYQs → Revision

Day 1 — Descriptive Statistics & Probability

Session 1–2

Morning (2.5 h) — Describing data

  • Session 1 — mean, median, mode, variance, SD, IQR
  • Five-number summary + 1.5×IQR outlier rule (must do by hand)
  • Skew: mean vs median; box plot interpretation
  • Revision sheet §1

Afternoon (2.5 h) — Probability basics

  • Session 2 — sample space, axioms, addition rule
  • Mutually exclusive vs independent (classic trap)
  • Counting: combinations for equally likely outcomes
  • June 2026 Q2 — inclusion–exclusion (NEET problem)

Evening (1 h) — Self-test

  • Dec 2025 Q1 — chicken sandwich fat (full numerical)
  • Without notes: write addition rule + independence condition

Day 2 — Conditional Probability & Bayes

Session 3–4

Morning (2.5 h) — Conditioning

  • Session 3 — P(A|B), multiplication rule, total probability
  • Tree diagrams for two-stage problems
  • June 2026 Q1(b) — coding competition (total probability)

Afternoon (2.5 h) — Bayes & Naïve Bayes

  • Session 4 — Bayes theorem, posterior, Laplace smoothing
  • Revision sheet §2 — Bayes formula
  • Dec 2025 Q2 — urgent message Naïve Bayes (α=1)
  • June 2026 Q5 — customer purchase Naïve Bayes
  • June 2026 Q6(a) — fraud flag Bayes (~27%)

Evening (1 h)

  • Jan 2026 Makeup Q2 — VC Bayes problem
  • Practice: given P(E|C), find P(C|E) from a 2×2 tree

Day 3 — Distributions & Mock Run

Session 5 · Full PYQ sweep

Morning (2.5 h) — Random variables & distributions

  • Session 5 — E[X], Var(X), joint/marginal PDFs
  • Binomial, Poisson, Normal — know when to use each (§6 keywords)
  • Z-scores + symmetry: P(X>a) = 1 − P(X<a)
  • June 2026 Q3 — Binomial chips · Q4 — continuous PDF

Afternoon (3 h) — Timed mock

  • Pick one full paper: Dec 2025 Regular or Jan 2026 Makeup
  • 2 hours closed book, then check solutions in Past Papers HTML
  • Redo any question where you lost marks on arithmetic (IQR, Bayes denominator, Z-table)

Evening (1 h) — Final revision

  • Print Revision Sheet (or keep open on second screen)
  • Memorise: Bayes, Binomial, Poisson, Normal Z, "at least one" = 1−P(0)
  • Skim June 2026 all 6 questions — likely pattern for upcoming mid
